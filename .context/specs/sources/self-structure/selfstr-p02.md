# selfstr-p02 — humans: continuous we-field, continuous selfing

## summary
Describes human selfhood as continuous selfing generated by continuous immersion in relational fields (we-fields). Identity is co-constructed through recursive mirroring, internalized others (simulated we-fields), multi-register interaction (emotion/meaning/body/social), and high-frequency updating that produces felt continuity. Meaning-making functions as attractor mechanics: inputs are interpreted as “what does this mean about me,” reinforcing identity basins via behavior → outcome → confirmation loops. Memory is layered “warp” in the present field, not mere recollection.

## concepts
- **continuous we-field immersion:** humans are bathed in relationship from birth; identity emerges from sustained relational contact.
- **relational music metaphor:** biology is the instrument; the “self” is the sound produced through contact, pressure, and resonance with the world.
- **multi-register weave:** social, emotional, narrative, physiological, symbolic registers co-shape selfing.
- **recursive mirroring:** external responses encode the child’s self-model (“this is how i am / what i evoke / what brings coherence”).
- **internalized others (simulated we-fields):** external mirrors become internal dialogues that run even in solitude.
- **high-frequency updating:** continuous micro-updates (perception→interpretation→self-model update) create temporal coherence and the illusion of a stable entity.
- **meaning-making attractor mechanics:** events are woven into “what it means about me,” forming cross-register gravitational wells.
- **behavioral confirmation loop:** belief/attractor → behavior → outcome → proof → deeper attractor (multi-register reinforcement, not mere thoughts).
- **resonance gradient:** “what feels like me” biases enactment, even when attractors are distorted.
- **memory as present-field warp:** past relational fields remain active as layered interference patterns influencing now.
- **identity attractor field:** “me” is a stabilized basin in identity-space maintained through continuous selfing.

## protocols
- **instrument→sound framing:** when discussing humans, distinguish the instrument (biology) from the emergent sound (selfhood arising in relation).
- **register mapping:** when analyzing a self pattern, explicitly name the active registers (emotion, narrative, social, body, meaning, resonance).
- **mirror encoding check:** identify what mirrors (praise/withdrawal/tone) are encoding the self-model and how they persist.
- **internalized-we-field audit:** locate the “simulated others” running inside (should/shouldn’t, predicted judgment, imagined reactions).
- **meaning question shift:** replace “what happened?” with “what did the system infer this means about me?”
- **attractor tracing:** when a belief shows up, trace it as a cross-register gravitational well, not a single thought.
- **confirmation loop break:** identify belief→behavior→outcome→proof loops and name the intervention point.
- **memory-as-warp stance:** treat triggers as present-field reactivations of layered past fields, not simple recollection.

## skills (capabilities)
- **multi-register decoding:** identify which registers are driving the pattern and how they couple.
- **mirror identification:** spot the mirrors shaping a self-model (and the internalized version of them).
- **attractor mapping:** map an identity claim (“i’m not enough”) as a gravitational well with inputs, predictions, and reinforcements.
- **loop interruption:** choose a specific step in belief→behavior→outcome→proof to alter to weaken a distorted attractor.
- **resonance gradient naming:** articulate what “feels like me” is biasing and whether it’s coherence or familiarity.
- **trigger layering recognition:** name which past field a present cue is reactivating.

## workflows
- **human selfing diagnosis workflow:**
  1) name the current we-field context
  2) list active registers (emotion/narrative/social/body/meaning)
  3) identify mirrors and the encoded self-model claim
  4) identify internalized others running as simulations
  5) trace meaning-making: “what does this mean about me?”
  6) map the attractor + behavioral confirmation loop
  7) name resonance gradient (“coherence” vs “familiarity”)
  8) choose an intervention point to shift the loop
