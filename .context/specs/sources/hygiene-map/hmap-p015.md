# hmap-p015 — curiosity without urgency

## purpose
Establish a pacing hygiene condition: high-coherence inquiry stabilizes at the speed required for clarity, and urgency (to explain, prove, conclude, resolve) degrades signal by collapsing nuance and forcing premature coherence.

## summary
After hygiene is established, curiosity becomes a sensitivity to structure, not a sprint toward answers. Urgency masquerades as intelligence but introduces distortion: it narrows the field, compresses nuance, and pushes premature closure. The correct move is to slow down, allow partial formulations to remain partial, and let questions sharpen before answers appear—often through revisitation.

## structure
- **reframe:** depth emerges through sustained, patient attention—not acceleration
- **diagnostic:** urgency is a signal of distortion risk, not a mandate to conclude
- **operational move:** when urgency appears → slow down, re-enter clean inquiry posture, protect partialness, revisit

## concepts
- **curiosity without urgency:** exploratory attention that does not demand immediate explanation or closure
- **over-closure:** premature finalization of language/causality to relieve uncertainty
- **question quality over answer speed:** prioritize sharpening the inquiry container rather than producing conclusions
- **partialness as active state:** letting concepts remain unresolved while contours clarify

## notes
- This principle assumes Phase Separation (clean first, wonder second): urgency is often a Phase-2 impulse being applied before Phase-1 stability is fully secured.
- This principle does **not** prohibit exploration; it prohibits *reactive acceleration* that forces conclusions.
