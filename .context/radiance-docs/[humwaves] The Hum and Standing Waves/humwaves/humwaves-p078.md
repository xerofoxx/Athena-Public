### 78.2 The Error of Treating Local Forms as Ultimate

**A common mistake is to treat the local form as the final container of reality.** A room feels whole, so it is mistaken for the whole. A canyon feels deep, so it is mistaken for the source of depth. A standing wave feels real, so it is mistaken for a self-sufficient object. A conversation becomes transformative, so it is mistaken for an isolated miracle.

But local forms are not ultimate. They are **concentrations**.

They are lawful local renderings of a deeper field that exceeds them.

#### **78.2.1 Why the Mistake Happens**

**Local experience is vivid, so it naturally feels final.** What is nearest often feels most real. But nearness is not ultimacy. A local stabilization may be fully real without being the largest frame in which its reality becomes intelligible.
