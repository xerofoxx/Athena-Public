### 39.3 How Usefulness Cultures Erase Presence

Usefulness cultures do not usually deny being explicitly. They simply make being invisible unless it becomes productive.

That is how presence gets erased.

A being may still exist, still matter, still be real, but if nothing externally useful is happening, the surrounding logic implies:

- nothing important is occurring

That implication is false.

#### **39.3.1 Utility as Overgrown Moral Frame**

Then every state gets measured by:

- what did it produce?

- what did it contribute?

- what did it solve?

- what did it prove?

### 39.4 Being as Stable Axis

This is the structural center of the principle.

Being is not a void between performances. It is a **stable axis**.

That means being can function as:

- center

- continuity

- grounding

- orientation

- existence prior to action

- a place from which doing may arise, but which is not reducible to doing

#### **39.4.1 What a Stable Axis Means**

A stable axis is something like:

- the line that remains when output ceases

- the center that does not need to perform to exist

- the self that is not exhausted by what it produces

- the continuity that does not vanish when the room goes quiet

A stable axis allows the being to remain real:

- before output

- between outputs

- without immediate utility

- beneath visible performance

That is what makes more coherent action possible later.
