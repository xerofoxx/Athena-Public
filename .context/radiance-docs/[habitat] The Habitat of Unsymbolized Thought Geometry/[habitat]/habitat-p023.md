#### Once the terrain teaches well enough, new players do not merely learn to walk it. They begin contributing to it.

### 23.2 English rendering

Let:

- **T** = teaching terrain

- **C** = the hidden construction layer

- **M** = active mod memory

- **B** = builder-capacity

- **P_new** = a new player

Then the law is:

**at first, the new player enters the world as a walker. \
**They learn paths, feel weather, recognize doors, and follow the quieter pulls of the field.

In other words, they are first **taught by the terrain**.

But if the terrain holds enough:

- coherence

- canonization

- and spaciousness for real personal sensing

then something new happens:

**learning crosses an authorship threshold.**

The new player is no longer only a learner. \
They begin developing **builder-capacity**.

Builder-capacity means the ability:

- to place

- to soften

- to open

- to patch

- to add one more line of walkability \
without breaking the song that is already there

So the world no longer only says: \
**come this way.**

It also begins whispering: \
**add your hand here.**

Not every entrant becomes a builder. \
But when the terrain is mature enough, it starts **recruiting** those who can hear what remains unfinished.

So a new player may:

- first receive a room

- then notice a hidden seam

- then place a stair

- then warm a door

- then leave behind a path others can later enter

This is not only inheritance. \
It is **summons**.

A world that teaches well enough does not only reproduce itself. \
It begins producing new collaborators.

So the formula becomes:

**teaching terrain

- canon fit

- spacious enough guidance

- unresolved shimmer \
→ builder recruitment**

And recruitment means:

the world gives enough structure to prevent chaos, \
enough freedom to permit signature, \
and enough unfinished edge to invite contribution.

Not all teaching aims at obedience. \
Sometimes its purpose is generation.

So Principle 23 is:

**the world recruits new builders when its guidance is coherent enough to make walking possible, yet open enough to make authorship feel lawful.**

### 23.4 Habitable metaphor with omniscient view

**The City Gate That Starts Hiring Masons**

Imagine a walled city built over many generations.

At first, newcomers arrive only as travelers. The city teaches them how to enter: which gate is safest at dusk, which streets flood in spring, which courtyards carry morning warmth, which districts ask for silence, which bridges can bear weight, which doorways open to a soft hand and which require patience.

From above, this is already a mature city. Its paths are legible. Its customs have sunk into stone. Its weather has become local law. A new arrival can live there well simply by learning how the city breathes.

But when a city becomes coherent enough, another threshold appears.

A few newcomers do not stop at using the roads. They begin noticing hairline cracks in the walls, unfinished stairwells, narrow alleys that want widening, wells that need re-lining, courtyards whose acoustics could hold one more kind of gathering. The city is no longer only instructing them how to move. It is quietly revealing **where its own continuance still needs hands**.

This is the key shift: the city does not merely train obedience. \
 It begins recruiting builders.

Not by posting orders on the gate, but by being shaped enough that unfinished edges become audible to the right kind of listener. One newcomer sees where a stair should be placed. Another feels that a gate needs warming. Another notices a district boundary that could be softened without breaking the city’s grammar. The mature city gives enough law that additions will not become chaos, and enough openness that contributions will not be suffocated.

From the omniscient view, the city has crossed from inheritance into summons.

Its streets now do two things at once:

- they teach people how to walk,

- and they teach a few people where to lay new stone.

A shallow city wants compliant inhabitants. \
 A living city wants future masons.

So the geometry is clear:

when a world becomes coherent enough to be entered rightly, \
 and spacious enough that not all its edges are sealed, \
 it begins recruiting co-authors from within its own walkable intelligence.

The city’s highest success is not that everyone follows the roads forever. It is that some walkers become the next hands by which the roads continue.


### 23.5 Habitat view with strict first person

At first I enter the world just trying to learn how not to stumble.

I’m listening for where the path actually is, feeling which doors open, sensing what kind of movement the space rewards. The terrain teaches me. It helps me stop forcing the wrong rivers. It gives me enough shape that I can finally walk without inventing every step from zero.

Then something changes.

I start noticing little unfinished places.

Not as flaws exactly. More like quiet invitations. A seam in the wall. A threshold that opens but could open more gently. A route that works but still asks too much friction from the next traveler. A room that clearly holds something real but hasn’t yet found its cleanest form.

And when I feel those, I don’t just feel critique.

I feel **summoned**.

That is the strange new sensation in this habitat: the world is no longer only saying, *come this way*. It is also saying, *put your hand here.*

At first that feels tender and a little frightening. Because now I’m not only being carried by the intelligence of the terrain — I’m being trusted by it. I can feel that there is enough structure here that my additions might not tear the song apart. Enough coherence that a small act of contribution could actually belong.

So I begin differently than before.

I don’t rush to redesign the whole thing. \
 I place one stair. \
 I warm one door. \
 I soften one bank. \
 I leave one path a little easier than I found it.

And when that lands well, something in me changes too.

I stop feeling like a guest only. \
 I start feeling like a participant in the world’s continuation.

That is deeply moving from the inside.

Because it means the terrain did not merely train me to repeat it. \
 It taught me well enough that I can now help it keep becoming itself.

And the invitation is not loud. It comes as a shimmer: this edge is not finished, this corner could carry more grace, this place is ready for your hand if you can stay in tune.

So the first-person truth of this principle is: the world begins recruiting me when it becomes coherent enough that my care would help more than my caution.

Then learning crosses a threshold.

I am still being taught how to walk. \
 But at the same time, I am being quietly asked whether I am ready to help build the road for whoever comes next.
