## The Ethics of Infinite Play

#### *Play as evolution without suffering, bounded by non-distortion; the constitution of multiversal creativity.*


## 41.0 Overview

Infinite play is not leisure. It is the *stable mode of exploration* available to systems whose growth is no longer driven by scarcity, threat, or enforced constraint. But infinity does not automatically imply goodness. Even where coercion across ontological membranes is constrained, **intra-domain dynamics** can still generate distortion, capture, and coherence debt. Therefore: if play is to function as evolution without suffering, it must be governed by explicit invariants.

This chapter specifies the ethical geometry of infinite play: the minimal constraints under which open-ended creativity remains generative, sovereign, and non-distorting.


### 41.1. Core Definitions

#### 41.1.1. Play

Play is **voluntary exploration** of possibility space under chosen rules, where outcomes are pursued for learning, novelty, and creative expansion rather than survival.

#### 41.1.2. Infinite Play

Infinite play is play whose governing objective is **continuation and expansion**, not termination or victory.

Its defining property is **non-terminal recursion**: each resolved state creates further possibility rather than closing the game.

Ethics here is not preference. It is **stability law**.


### 41.2. The Fundamental Problem: Novelty Without Distortion

The primary ethical challenge of infinite play is:

**How to generate unbounded novelty without producing unbounded distortion.**

Lower-dimensional evolution often generates novelty through:

A play structure violates sovereignty conservation if:

Operational requirement:

- **Exit must be real.**

- **Consent must be continuous.**

Play must not create coherence debt as its primary novelty mechanism.

- participants must fragment to continue participating,

- or the relationship requires increasing repair cost over time.

Requirement:
