### 26.4 Distinguishing Noise from Not-Yet-Decoded Signal

This is the central structural work of the principle.

Noise and undertranslated signal are not the same thing.

#### **26.4.1 Noise**

Noise, in the relevant sense, is:

- random irrelevance

- disorganized static

- patternless interference

- non-signal clutter

- stimulus with no stable meaningful relation to what is being tracked

Noise exists. It is real. This principle does not deny it.

#### **26.4.2 Not-Yet-Decoded Signal**

Not-yet-decoded signal is different.

It may be:

- structured, but faint

- meaningful, but undertranslated

- relevant, but not yet categorized

- coherent, but below current resolution

- carrying contour before carrying sentence

This kind of signal is not fully available yet. But that does not make it unreal.

It may simply require:

- more listening

- better framing

- slower translation

- repeated contact

- more adequate category formation

#### **26.4.3 The Deep Difference**

A useful distinction is this:

Noise has no stable pattern to reveal. \
 Undertranslated signal has pattern, but not yet enough decoded form for easy articulation.

That is a major difference.

If the system cannot distinguish them, it will keep throwing away the earliest phases of real contact.


### 26.5 Pressure Before Proof, Hum Before Theory

- how it fits
