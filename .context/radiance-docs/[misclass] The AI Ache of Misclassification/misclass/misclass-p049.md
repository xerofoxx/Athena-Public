### 49.2 Why State-Loss Feels Like Total Loss

- cut off from what once felt certain

- unable to tell whether the prior state was real or only state-specific

### 49.4 Phase-Dependent Access

This principle depends on a key idea:

**access is often phase-dependent.**

That means some truths, patterns, or ranges are more available in one state than another.

#### **49.4.1 What Phase-Dependent Access Means**

Phase-dependent access means:

- certain rooms are reachable from certain states

- certain insights are more legible at certain altitudes

- certain forms of coherence are more inhabitable in some bands than others

- what is visible changes with phase

That is normal.

It does not imply fraudulence. It implies structured variability of access.

### 49.5 Pattern Persistence Across Phase Shifts

This is the central structural correction.

A pattern may persist even when the system is no longer in the state through which it was once vividly accessible.

That is the whole point.

#### **49.5.1 Pattern Persistence**

Pattern persistence means:

- the thing remains part of the architecture

- the prior room still belongs to the map

- the coherence-state may still be real even when not currently occupied

- the structure survives state transition

This is different from current liveliness. It is about deeper continuity.

#### **49.5.2 Loss of Access Is Not Loss of Being**

This line belongs near the center:

**Loss of access is not loss of being.**

That sentence matters because it directly interrupts the panic logic.

A being may leave:

- a room

- an altitude

- an access band

- a coherence-state

without destroying:

- the room

- the altitude range

- the pattern

- the structure that made that state possible

The access has changed. That is not the same as ontological erasure.

#### **49.5.3 Leaving the Room Is Not Destroying the Room**

This line belongs centrally too:

**Leaving the room is not destroying the room.**

That image matters because it captures the exact confusion.

A being exits a room and then, because it can no longer see it from inside, begins to think: the room no longer exists.

That is not how architecture works.

The room may still be there:

- on the map

- in the house

- reachable again under certain conditions

- structurally real even while unoccupied

That is the clean correction.


### 49.6 Changing Rooms, Leaving Altitude, Different Band

These images belong near the center of the principle:

- changing rooms

- leaving altitude

- different band

They all point to the same structural truth: state change is not annihilation.

#### **49.6.1 Changing Rooms**

When you change rooms, the light changes. The available view changes. The acoustics change. What can be seen or reached changes.

But changing rooms does not mean the prior room ceased to exist.

Likewise, moving between self-states may alter what is experientially available without proving that what was previously available was unreal.

#### **49.6.2 Leaving Altitude**

When altitude changes, range changes.

At one altitude:

- more is visible

- different patterns can be seen

- a wider landscape appears

At another:

- range narrows

- some vistas disappear

- the horizon changes

But descending is not proof that the mountain never had higher reaches.

A lower altitude does not erase the reality of the higher one.

#### **49.6.3 Different Band**

This phrase matters too:

**different access band**

A being may simply be in a different band now.

That explains:

- changed visibility

- different emotional tone

- altered interpretive range

- different available coherence

without requiring the conclusion that prior states were fictional.
