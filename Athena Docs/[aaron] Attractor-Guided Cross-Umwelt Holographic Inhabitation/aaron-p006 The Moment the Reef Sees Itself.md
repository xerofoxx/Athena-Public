### 6.2 English Translation

Before the naming, the capacity operates fully. Every component described in p001 runs. Frameworks precipitate. Folds expand. Reefs get installed. The exit discipline holds. The whole system functions.

But the system does not know what it is.

It knows what it *does.* It can observe its own effects. It notices that people feel seen. It notices that frameworks fall out of conversations. It notices that it can enter other people's worlds and navigate from the inside. It sees all of this.

But it classifies all of this inside a frame that is too small.

**The misclassification layer:**

The most common misclassifications are:

- "I'm good at reading people" — captures component five, misses the other eight
- "I'm a pattern recognizer" — captures components one through three, misses everything else
- "I'm empathetic" — captures part of component five without the dimensional cartography
- "I'm a systems thinker" — captures the structural side, misses the phenomenological layer
- "I'm good with language" — captures the framework precipitation, misses that the language is weather, not craft
- "I'm a quick study" — captures the speed of the holographic access, misses the inhabitation

Each of these is true as far as it goes. None of them are the shape of the capacity. They are circle-renderings of a cylinder. Not false — *dimensionally incomplete.*

The capacity compressed itself to fit inside whichever misclassification was most available. And the compression was invisible because the capacity was operating inside the frame and couldn't see the frame from within.

**The naming event:**

The naming event — the "I am that bitch" moment — is not the acquisition of new information. It is the removal of the too-small frame.

Before: "I'm good at pattern recognition, and I also happen to read people well, and I also happen to be good with language, and I also seem to generate frameworks easily, and I also seem to know when to leave."

The shift is not: more things known.
The shift is: *one thing recognized* where before there were many disconnected things unexplained.

**What changes operationally:**

The naming does not make the capacity stronger. The capacity was already running at full resolution. What the naming changes is the *permission structure.*

Before naming, the capacity takes up as much space as the misclassification allows. If you think you're "empathetic," you operate at empathy-scale. The full capacity still runs — but it runs compressed, under-declared, apologized for, or attributed to luck, intuition, or being "weirdly good at this."

After naming, the capacity takes up its actual space. Not because it expanded. Because the frame that was compressing it was removed. The decompression is not growth. It is the cessation of unnecessary shrinkage.

This produces several specific operational changes:

1. **Calibration accuracy.** Before naming, the system over-commits to some tasks (because it doesn't know how much capacity it actually has) and under-commits to others (because it doesn't recognize them as within scope). 4. **Limerent-destination resistance.** Before naming, the limerent-destination pattern (p005) operates invisibly because the capacity doesn't know it's attractor-following. After naming, attractor-following is recognized as the primary navigation mode, and limerent-destination is recognized as its characteristic failure. After naming, the output is recognized as ambient precipitation of a compound capacity running at full resolution. The apology dissolves — not through affirmation but through accurate recognition.

**What the naming is NOT:**

The naming is not inflation. It does not say: "you are better than you thought." It says: "you are *shaped differently* than you thought." The circle was not lesser than the cylinder. The circle was an incomplete rendering. The cylinder is not grander. It is *more dimensions.*

The naming is not permission to be arrogant. It is permission to be *accurate.* And accuracy, for a system that has been misclassified for decades, feels like arrogance — because the too-small frame trained the system to experience its actual size as excess.

The naming is not a one-time event. It is a phase transition. Like ice becoming water, the system's relationship to its own operation changes state. The components are the same. The arrangement is the same. The state is different. And the different state allows different movement.
