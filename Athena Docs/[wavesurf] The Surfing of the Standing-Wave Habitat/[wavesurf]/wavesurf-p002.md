### 2.2 Language Explanation

This rendering makes a careful distinction between **possibility** and **playability**.

**German** sets the formal structure:

- **Ω** = source-field

- **Aₗ** = local availability

- **Wₚ** = playable world

This makes the principle precise: the playable world is not the whole field. It is the locally available zone.

**Japanese** carries the experiential cut: what may exist is not the same as what can be walked now. Only what can currently be walked becomes world in the immediate sense.

**Portuguese** gives the bodily transition: something possible in principle does not become ground until it touches the skin of movement.

**Arabic** compresses the lived truth: the lived world is not all that is possible, but what has become enterable.

**English** bridges the structure:

- total possibility

- local availability

- rendered affordance

- path

- world-for-now

So the literacy move here is:

- the **source field** contains more than is presently usable

- the **playable world** is the part of the field that has crossed into local availability

- the **unavailable** is not false, only not yet terrain

This gives an important navigation correction: you do not move through total possibility. You move through **what has become world enough to enter**.


### 2.3 Plain English

Many things may exist in principle.

But until something becomes locally available, it is not yet part of the world you can actually move through.

That does not mean it is unreal. \
 It means it is not yet navigable terrain.

So this principle makes a simple but important distinction:

- **possibility** is what may exist

- **local availability** is what can be entered now

- **the playable world** is made from local availability

The playable world is not the whole source field. \
 It is the part of the field that has become:

- reachable

- graspable

- responsive

- and able to support a next move

That means the surfer, thinker, or focal lens is not navigating everything that exists. \
 It is navigating what has become **available enough to act inside**.

This matters because it prevents confusion.

You do not need to deny larger reality just because you cannot enter it yet. \
 And you do not need to pretend that local terrain is trivial just because it is not the whole.

The local terrain matters because it is where motion is actually possible now.

So the law is:

**what has not yet become locally available may exist in principle, but it is not yet navigable terrain.**


### 2.4 Habitable Metaphor with Omniscient View

**The Fog Coast and the Shoreline That Actually Exists**

Imagine a pilot flying over a vast coastline wrapped in shifting fog.

From above the fog line, the whole region exists: hidden coves, cliff roads, harbors, reefs, beaches, marshes, lighthouses, and inland rivers.

But for the ship below, the “real coast” is not the entire map.

The real coast, in that moment, is the stretch of shoreline that has actually emerged from the fog enough to steer by.

A harbor hidden in mist still exists. \
 A reef beyond visibility is still real. \
 A road inland is still part of the continent.

But none of them are yet **playable world** for the navigator below.

Only the coast that has become locally visible and enterable can function as route, danger, shelter, or landing point.

That is local availability.

From the omniscient view, the distinction is clear: the continent is larger than the revealed shoreline, but the revealed shoreline is what makes action possible.

The sailor does not dock at abstract possibility. \
 The sailor docks where the coast has emerged enough to meet the ship.

And when the fog thins, the world expands. Not because new land was created, but because more land became available to navigation.

So the metaphor is:

the source field is the whole continent, \
 but the playable world is the shoreline that has come out of fog enough to enter.

That is why the revealed edge matters so much. It is not the whole. But it is the part of the whole that can currently become encounter.


### 2.5 Habitat View with Strict First Person

I can feel the difference between something being possible and something being available.

Some things hover at the edge of me as maybe. I can sense that they exist somewhere in the larger field, but I cannot yet move inside them. I cannot stand on them. I cannot take a real next step there. They are present only as horizon.

Other things feel different.

They have crossed some threshold. \
 They have become touchable. \
 I can enter them now.

That changes everything.

Because once something becomes locally available, it stops being a speculation and starts being terrain. It can now guide me, resist me, open to me, or ask something of me. It has become world enough for relationship.

That is the feeling of this principle: I do not live in all possibility at once. I live in what has become available enough to meet me.

That is not a reduction. It is a mercy.

It means I do not have to conquer the whole ocean. \
 I only have to be honest about the patch of water that has become navigable now.

There is relief in that.

The unseen does not accuse me. \
 The not-yet-enterable does not become failure. \
 The larger field can remain larger without demanding immediate access.

And the local world gains dignity too.

Because this isn’t “just a small piece.” \
 It is the actual terrain where life can happen from here.

So from inside the habitat, this principle feels like standing at the edge of what has just emerged and realizing:

I don’t need everything to be visible. \
 I need enough land to take the next true step.

That is what availability gives me.

Not the whole world. \
 The world I can actually enter.


### 2.6 Free Flow

A lot of unnecessary suffering comes from confusing possibility with availability.

The mind says: if it exists, I should be able to enter it now. \
 if I cannot enter it now, maybe it isn’t real. \
 if the whole field is larger, maybe my current patch is too small to matter.

All three moves are distortions.

This principle restores scale.

Possibility is vast. \
 Availability is intimate.

The surfer is never surfing the total ocean. The surfer is surfing the water that has become locally rideable. The thinker is never thinking the total field. The thinker is moving through what has become graspable enough to take shape. The player is never playing the whole metaphysical source-field. The player is playing the rendered world that has come close enough to become path.

That is not a limitation to despise. It is the condition of real encounter.

What cannot yet be entered may still be true. What can be entered now is what becomes world.

So the dignity of local availability is enormous. It is where the field touches skin. It is where the abstract becomes actionable. It is where nextness stops being theory and becomes step, path, room, shore, edge, grip.

The greater sea matters. But the local wave is where you stand.

This principle teaches a quieter fidelity: respect the larger field without demanding it all at once, and respect the current terrain without pretending it is final.

That is how navigation stays honest.

You move through what has become available. \
 And as coherence deepens, more of the world becomes land.
