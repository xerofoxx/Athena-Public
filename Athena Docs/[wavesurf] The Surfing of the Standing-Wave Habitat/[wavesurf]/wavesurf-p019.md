#### Preference is where prior successful movement has softened the banks.

### 19.2 Language Explanation

This rendering defines **preference** as a history-shaped ease of entry rather than a purely arbitrary personal taste.

**German** gives the structural skeleton:

- **A** = attractor region

- **P** = preference

- **B** = bank-softening

- **X** = prior successful crossings

- **E** = ease of entry

This lets the principle say something exact: preference forms when repeated successful crossings soften the approach to an attractor.

**Japanese** carries the phenomenological correction: liking is not originally “just subjective.” It is the feeling that a place repeatedly entered without strain has become the more natural way.

**Portuguese** gives the beautiful riverbank image: taste leans where the bank has already been softened by crossings that worked.

**Chinese** sharpens the developmental sequence: you do not first decide that you like something. First there are many clean entries; later that region starts feeling like where your movement naturally goes.

**German** then adds a key intimacy: preference is often the experience that “the field already knows me here.”

**Arabic** carries the tactile leaning: the eye bends toward the place whose bank has grown softer from prior passage.

**Sanskrit** compresses the sequence: successful passage, then bank-softening, then easier entry, then taste.

**English** bridges the entire logic:

- repeated fit

- softened banks

- lowered entry cost

- faster return

- quicker recognition

- preference as worn access to an attractor

So the literacy move here is:

- preference is not merely decorative liking

- preference forms where prior crossings have made future access easier

- the system later experiences that ease as “this is where I naturally lean”

- preference is therefore path-history felt as affinity

This principle teaches how attraction gets shaped by repeated viable contact.


### 19.3 Plain English

Preference is often where the path has already been made easier.

That means you do not always prefer something because you randomly like it. Sometimes you prefer it because prior successful movement through that region has lowered the cost of entering it again.

If a certain:

- mood

- style

- symbol system

- conceptual region

- atmosphere

- or way of thinking

has repeatedly welcomed you cleanly, then its “banks” get softer.

That means:

- return gets easier

- hesitation decreases

- recognition happens faster

- entry feels more natural

And eventually that natural ease gets named: **preference**

So preference is not always a primary cause. \
 Sometimes it is a later surface label for a deeper field-history.

This is why some things feel like:

- “my kind of music”

- “my kind of image”

- “my kind of room”

- “my kind of thought”

- “my kind of world”

The feeling of “mine” may partly come from the fact that the route into that attractor has already been worn by successful prior crossings.

So the law is:

**preference is where prior successful movement has softened the banks.**

It is worn attractor access.


### 19.4 Habitable Metaphor with Omniscient View

**The Riverbank Where the Herd Crosses Easily**

Imagine a wide river running through a valley.

There are many possible places to cross. Some banks are steep and muddy. Some are tangled with roots. Some look promising from afar but collapse under hoof. Some are rocky enough to injure. Some are so sharp that even successful crossings feel costly.

But one bend in the river has a different quality.

The slope is gentler there. The mud is firmer. Stones lie under the water in a way that supports weight. One animal crosses successfully. Then another. Then the herd begins returning to the same place.

From above, the pattern is easy to see.

Each successful crossing changes the bank a little: grass bends down, mud compacts, the route becomes clearer, the waterline grows familiar, the entry angle gets easier.

Soon the herd seems to “prefer” that crossing.

But the preference is not mysterious.

It is the felt result of softened access.

The crossing works, so it gets used. \
 Because it gets used, it works even better. \
 Because it works even better, it starts feeling natural.

That is the law.

The herd is not merely expressing abstract taste. It is responding to a place where past successful movement has made future movement easier.

So the riverbank becomes a visible metaphor for preference: not random liking, but a route where arrival has become less costly through repeated truth of crossing.

From the omniscient view, preference is the valley remembering where movement can pass without waste.


### 19.5 Habitat View with Strict First Person

I can feel when something is not just attractive, but easier for me to enter.

That difference matters.

Some things catch my eye but still require a fight. Some things look good from a distance but resist me when I arrive. Some things are interesting, yet the crossing always costs more than the shimmer first promised.

Then there are the other regions.

The ones where I can feel that the bank has already been softened.

A sentence-style, an atmosphere, a kind of music, a symbolic texture, a thought-region, a visual weather — when I approach it, I do not have to force as much. The entry is cleaner. My body knows where to place its weight. The field receives me with less friction.

That is what preference feels like from inside.

Not merely: I like this.

More like: I know how to arrive here, and here knows how to receive me.

That is deeply different.

It means preference has memory in it. The route has been used before. Some of the work of approach has already been done. I am not inventing the crossing from zero every time.

So when I say I prefer something, I can now feel the topological truth under the statement: the banks are softer here.

That softness carries a lot: less hesitation, quicker trust, faster recognition, more immediate world-opening.

And because of that, the region starts feeling “like me,” even if what that really means is simply: this is one of the places where movement between me and the world has repeatedly gone well enough to become easier.

From inside, this principle feels gentle.

It tells me that preference is not always arbitrary, and not always essence either. It can be history becoming ease.

It can be many true arrivals slowly making one region feel more and more like home.


### 19.6 Free Flow

Preference is often discussed as though it begins at the level of declaration.

I like this. \
 I don’t like that. \
 This is my style. \
 That isn’t me.

But life usually builds those statements from underneath.

Long before the declaration, there were crossings. Some costly, some awkward, some sterile, some tearing. And some clean.

Clean enough that the next return cost less. Clean enough that hesitation dropped. Clean enough that the world opened instead of splintering.

That is where preference begins.

Not as abstract opinion, but as the field learning where approach can happen with less unnecessary violence. Over time, the system bends toward those softened entries. The easier arrival feels good. The clean receiving feels good. The familiar bank grows more legible. And eventually all of that gets compressed into a single surface report: I prefer this.

That’s why preference can be trustworthy without being absolute. It often points to where coherence has already proven itself metabolizable. It says: this route has held my weight before. This crossing has nourished rather than shredded. This attractor has a bank I can enter with less waste.

That is not trivial.

It means preference can be read as topology. As the visible shape of accumulated successful contact. As worn access to a region of the world that has repeatedly opened rather than resisted.

And that makes preference less shallow than it first appears.

Not destiny. \
 Not final identity. \
 Not arbitrary whim.

Just this: the field remembers where arrival got easier, and begins to lean there again.

That is one of the deepest truths of preference:

it is not only what you choose. \
 It is where history has already made choosing cost less.
