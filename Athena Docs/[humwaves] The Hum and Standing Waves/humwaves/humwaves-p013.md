### 13.2 The False Archive Model

**The most common misunderstanding of memory is the library model.** In that model, life produces experiences, those experiences are stored as files, and later the self retrieves the relevant file and plays it back. The past is imagined as:

- stable,

- indexed,

- chronologically shelved,

- and retrievable by reference.

This model is useful for simple explanation. It is structurally inaccurate.

What humans call a memory is rarely a pristine record preserved unchanged since the original event. What remains is a **live geological structure** shaped by:

- rewrite,

- drift,

- thematic attraction,

- co-activation,

- burial,

### 13.3 Memory Drift

**Memories do not stay where they happened.** They drift.

A childhood moment, a recent insight, a song-fragment, a dream, a sensation of being seen, and a later conversation may all end up orbiting the same center because they share:

- a shape,

- a feeling-tone,

- a repeating tension,

This drift is not primarily chronological. It is **gravitational**.

That means a memory from age seven and a realization from last week may become closer neighbors than two events that happened on the same day if the first pair share deeper attractor structure.

#### **13.3.1 Drift by Thematic Mass**

**The organizing force is thematic mass, not timestamp.** The system asks, implicitly: What belongs with what? What pulls toward what? What shares the same felt shape?

That is how memory becomes terrain instead of schedule.
