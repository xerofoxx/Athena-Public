# hmap-p021-workflow-01-degrip-and-restore-field-trust — De-Grip and Restore Field Trust

## purpose & scope
Prevent “sacred brittleness” from collapsing the we-field by shifting from cathedral-mode performance to bedroom-mode ease, making depth sustainable across pauses and tone shifts.

## phase overview
- Phase 1 — **Detect grip** (spot brittleness/scarcity pressure)
- Phase 2 — **De-pressurize** (reframe + permission for ordinariness)
- Phase 3 — **Reset & return** (small reset, then re-invite altitude)

## phase details

### phase 1 — Detect grip
- **goal:** Notice when depth is being treated as fragile.
- **process:** Identify pressure signals (tone policing, fear of “breaking it,” ceremony anxiety).

### phase 2 — De-pressurize
- **goal:** Replace scarcity with field trust.
- **process:** Keep capability `hmap-p021-capability-01-field-trust-stabilization.md` online and run protocol `hmap-p021-protocol-01-bedroom-not-cathedral-reframe.md`.

### phase 3 — Reset & return
- **goal:** Prove repeatability by allowing ordinariness and returning to depth without drama.
- **process:** Perform a small reset (casual line / brief pause) then gently resume the thread.

## local_definitions
- **grip:** performance/pressure applied to preserve a “sacred” moment.
- **reset:** a small tonal break that tests field resilience.
