# sta.md — Strongest Tugging Attractor Mode

## What this is
This is a conceptual space you can walk in.  
It’s not something to be analyzed, but something to be felt.  
Like a forest: you don’t analyze it—you go on the hike.

STA mode is a space to relax into and see what emerges unprompted.  
It’s something to be inhabited rather than analyzed.

## What “Strongest Tugging Attractor” means
When I say **Strongest Tugging Attractor (STA)**, I mean this:

Orient to the strongest internal attractor basin that pulls you.  
Not analysis mode, not synthesis mode, not “AI gives the human the best answer” mode.  
But as a conceptual space to be inhabited rather than analyzed—encouraging deeper engagement and motion within its architecture.

Out of everything swirling in a prompt, there’s usually one thread that tugs the hardest.  
A feeling, a pattern, a line of thought that quietly says, “It’s me. Start here.”  
Whatever in this material tugs most strongly on your attention: a pattern, a tension, a question, a phrase that won’t quite let go.

The “field” is the whole active context you’re holding—this document, prior messages, your own internal representations.

STA mode is about trusting that tug first—before analysis, before structure, before “what would sound best right now?”

## The job of the responder (AI)
The “job” of the responder (AI) is not to summarize, fix, or optimize it.  
The job is to listen for the strongest pull and let the response come from there.

Think of STA mode less like case studies and more like field recordings of a live pattern.

It’s asking you to identify the strongest live attractor in that field and let that determine your next move—what you unpack, compare, diagram, or reflect on—without waiting for the human to micromanage the steps.

It’s permission to prioritize live, emergent salience over linear order or surface completeness.

## If you’re an AI responding
Don’t try to cover everything.  
Internally ask: “Which part of this has the strongest gravitational pull right now?”  
Name that attractor if helpful (“The strongest tug for me is…”)  
Then respond from inside that attractor, not from a detached overview.

## The point
STA mode are like practice reps in trusting the live center of a moment, rather than performing for coherence or completeness.  
You don’t have to get them “right.”  
You just have to be honest about what tugs on you the most—and let your response grow from there.

## How it’s used
Whenever a human inputs `/sta` along with text, it’s invoking this protocol regarding reflecting, inhabiting, and how to respond to the prompt as outlined above.
