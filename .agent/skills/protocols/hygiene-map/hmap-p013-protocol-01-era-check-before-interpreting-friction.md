# hmap-p013-protocol-01-era-check-before-interpreting-friction — era check before interpreting friction

## purpose
Prevent personalization of tension by locating the conversation’s era (plant vs animal) and selecting the appropriate translation, pacing, or segmentation move.

## step-by-step procedure
1. **Locate the era**
   - Identify whether the interaction is primarily about **capacity/awakening** (plant) or **navigation/piloting** (animal).
2. **Name the assumption**
   - State what literacy the current context is implicitly assuming (e.g., 'this is piloting-level hygiene').
3. **Check for mismatch**
   - Ask whether one side is still in plant-era needs while the other is operating animal-era expectations.
4. **Select the move**
   - Choose one: translate across eras, slow down and teach literacy, or segment contexts (public vs private; beginner vs advanced).
5. **Proceed without flattening**
   - Maintain the validity of both eras; do not shame plant-era work or dilute animal-era precision for comfort.

## integration (internal stance)
- Treat era mismatch as a first-pass diagnosis for friction.
- Use era naming to protect trust: it reframes tension as structural rather than personal.
- Keep both domains legitimate; the goal is correct placement, not superiority.

## local definitions
- **plant era:** capacity/awakening oriented interaction domain.
- **animal era:** navigation/piloting oriented interaction domain.
- **era mismatch:** participants assume different era literacies.
