# hmap-p015-protocol-01 — slowdown and hold

## purpose
Reduce urgency-induced distortion by slowing tempo, protecting partialness, and re-centering the conversation on question quality until clarity stabilizes.

## step-by-step procedure
1. **spot urgency**
   - Identify cues: pressure to resolve, proof-demand, forced causality, “lock it in” language.
2. **name the mode shift**
   - Briefly mark: “Let’s slow down—something subtle is forming.”
3. **decompress**
   - Replace conclusion-seeking with 1–3 discriminating questions.
   - Narrow claims; widen time horizon.
4. **protect partialness**
   - Explicitly permit incompleteness: “We can keep this provisional.”
5. **choose one next move**
   - Either (a) clarify definitions/layers (return to hygiene), or (b) stay in hypothesis mode without resolving.
6. **invite revisitation**
   - Suggest a return pass once more signal accumulates: “Let’s revisit after we see it recur / after another angle.”

## integration (internal stance)
- Treat speed as a distortion vector.
- Trust return/revision cycles.
- Prioritize clean inquiry containers over impressive conclusions.

## local definitions
- **decompress:** expand conceptual breathing room by moving from answers → questions and from certainty → provisional framing.
