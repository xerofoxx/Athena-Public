# hmap-p014-capability-01-structure-motion-pairing — structure–motion pairing

## essence / core function
Hold **map-form clarity** and **enactment-form learning** as complementary, and prevent collapsing one into the other (structure-only brittleness or motion-only drift).

## when to use
- When a principle feels “true but unusable” in practice.
- When discussion becomes mystical/drifty because it lacks clear boundaries or language.
- When someone has lots of vocabulary but repeatedly mis-times or mis-applies the moves.

## inner stance / orientation
- Treat understanding as *navigation skill*, not only correct descriptions.
- Assume missingness is likely: “Which half (map or movement) is absent right now?”
- Stay non-shaming: lack of motion is not stupidity; lack of structure is not irresponsibility.

## mechanics / how it works
- **Structure** stabilizes: boundaries, terms, distinctions, constraints.
- **Motion** transmits: timing, pacing, felt sense, typical mistakes, recovery arcs.
- Pairing increases transfer: what is named in structure becomes *usable* through motion; what is felt in motion becomes *teachable* through structure.

## micro-skills
- Identify “structure-only” symptoms (brittle certainty; no application).
- Identify “motion-only” symptoms (strong felt sense; weak boundary control).
- Ask the diagnostic question: “Do we have a map but no lived example, or an example but no clean language?”
- Cross-reference: attach the nearest protocol/capability IDs to whatever is missing.

## local definitions
- **structure document:** principles/distinctions/taxonomies used for clean language and boundaries.
- **motion document:** stories/walkthroughs/dialogues used for timing and felt application.
- **brittle understanding:** clarity-as-vocabulary without navigational competence.
