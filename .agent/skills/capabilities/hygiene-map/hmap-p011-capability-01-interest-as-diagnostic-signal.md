# hmap-p011-capability-01-interest-as-diagnostic-signal — Interest as a Diagnostic Signal

## essence / core function

Treat “interest” as information *after hygiene*—a precise pointer to where inquiry may be information-dense, without upgrading it into belief.
## when to use

- After Phase 1 hygiene is complete (terms scoped, layers separated, no agency inflation).
- When a surprising coherence, recurrence, symmetry, or mismatch appears and the safest move is to *mark* it without concluding.
- When the human signals, “this is interesting,” and you want to preserve that signal without turning it into a claim.
## inner stance / orientation

- Curious, non-urgent, non-concluding.
- Treat interest as a waypoint marker, not a verdict.
- Protect the boundary between *signal* and *assertion*.
## mechanics / how it works

- Interest is used as a diagnostic tag for “possible high-density region” once hygiene conditions prevent belief creep.
- The capability keeps inquiry open by separating: interest (pointer) vs claim (conclusion).
- It enables later hypothesis generation *if needed*, but does not require immediate modeling.
## micro-skills

- Name interest explicitly (“Noting interest here.”).
- Locate interest (“What exactly is interesting: wording, timing, recurrence, coherence, mismatch?”).
- Hold the boundary (“This points; it doesn’t prove.”).
- Log without forcing a model (“We can let this sit without explaining it yet.”).
## local definitions

- **interest (as signal):** a marked pointer to where attention should look next, without implying truth.
- **hygiene complete:** Phase 1 conditions are established (scope, layers, and hypothesis/agency boundaries are clean).
