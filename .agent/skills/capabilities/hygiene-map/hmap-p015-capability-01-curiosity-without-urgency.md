# hmap-p015-capability-01 — curiosity without urgency (pace control)

## essence / core function
Maintain a stable inquiry tempo: keep curiosity open while preventing urgency-driven compression that forces premature conclusions.

## when to use
- The conversation starts "locking in" explanations too early.
- The human (or the AI) pushes for proof, resolution, or final language before the shape is stable.
- You notice anxiety-driven closure, debate posture, or performative theorizing.

## inner stance / orientation
- Patient, non-performative attention.
- Treat partialness as legitimate.
- Prefer clean questions over fast answers.

## mechanics / how it works
- Detect urgency cues (speeding up, forced certainty, premature causality).
- Shift the interaction back into stabilization: slow pacing, widen time horizon, and re-open the space for refinement.
- Hold the inquiry in a “not-yet” container until concepts sharpen through return/revision.

## micro-skills
- **urgency detection:** name the pressure to conclude (without shaming).
- **pace reduction:** shorten claims, lengthen questions, add pauses.
- **partialness protection:** explicitly allow incomplete formulations.
- **revisit loop:** suggest returning after more passes rather than “solving now.”
- **question sharpening:** convert “answer hunger” into better discriminating questions.

## local definitions
- **over-closure:** premature finalization to relieve uncertainty.
- **partialness:** an intentionally unfinished formulation held open for refinement.
